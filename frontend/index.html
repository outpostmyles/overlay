A pre-kickoff prediction sheet for every game, frozen ~75 minutes before kickoff (before lineups leak): the model's call on the match result plus total goals, each team's goals, both-teams-to-score, and total corners, with the de-vigged market line on the 1X2 frozen at the same instant as the benchmark. After the game every line is graded against the result, right or wrong. The 1X2 also scores the model against the market on calibration (does it add anything over the sharp line). Forward-only and never backfilled (the model has already seen the played games, so a backfill would be hindsight); penalties are out of scope (a level knockout grades as a draw); corners grade only with an API-Football key. Small sample, exploratory: the model-vs-market aggregate stays hidden until enough games settle, and none of this should be used to retune the model.

The de-vigged Polymarket futures (vig-free probabilities to reach each knockout round and to win the cup) are the sharp, primary read. Alongside each, an independent Monte Carlo simulation of the bracket (the match model played out thousands of times, conditioned on every group result so far) rides as a second opinion. The model is opponent-adjusted and graded on a leak-free holdout, but it reads tournament odds off long-run goal records, so it can diverge from the market in either direction; read large gaps as disagreement, not as edges to bet. The 12-group field is rebuilt from finished games, so it survives the group-winner markets closing.
